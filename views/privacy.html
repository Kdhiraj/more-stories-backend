USE OF COOKIES AND SIMILAR TECHNOLOGIES

  1. Cookies.

A cookie is a piece of information in the form of a very small text file that is placed on an internet user's hard drive. It is generated by a web page server, which is basically the computer that operates a web site. The information the cookie contains is set by the server and it can be used by that server whenever the user visits the site. A cookie can be thought of as an internet user's identification card, which tells a web site when the user has returned. Basically, cookies do not collect your PI, but only information about your browser session. For more information about cookies, please visit: http://www.aboutcookies.org.

If a cookie is placed by the website or online service you are using now, it is called a first-party cookie. If a cookie is placed by a website or online service other than the one you are using now, it is called a third party cookie.

We use first-party cookies within the App to personalize your experience with the App, to understand and save your preferences for future visits, and to carry out other tasks relating to the operation or improvement of the App.

We do not use cookies to access information on your device.

All recent versions of popular browsers allow users to control cookies. Users can set their browsers to either accept or reject all or certain cookies. Users can also set their browser to notify them each time a cookie is offered.

However, in order to ensure compliance with the European Union regulations, we place fixed notifications about cookies (We have placed the cookies on your device to help make our App better. You can change your cookie settings at any time. If you continue without changing your settings, we’ll assume that you consent to receive all cookies with our App) at all pages until you click on “Continue”. By clicking on “Continue” you give your express content for the use of cookies.

Users can also control Local Shared Objects on their computer, also known as LSOs or Flash cookies, but not with their browsers. For example, Adobe's website offers tools to control Flash cookies on your computer. Users of the Firefox browser can also get an add-on to detect and delete Flash cookies. Please note that Flash cookies and HTML cookies are different in terms of deleting and removal. For more information about Flash cookies, please visit: https://helpx.adobe.com/flash-player/kb/disable-local-shared-objects-flash.html,
